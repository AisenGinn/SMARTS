ULTRA Competition

Overview

Background

Self-driving cars have been a hot topic in recent years, and is a thriving and booming research area holding the promise of a new generation in transportation development. Intending to further research in autonomous driving and in identifying rising stars, HUAWEI is hosting this competition using the open-source ULTRA platform.

Environment

ULTRA, is an open-source Reinforcement Learning (RL) framework for autonomous driving built upon the SMARTS simulator. It provides a generalization benchmark for RL driving agents undergoing intersection navigation, specifically in unprotected left turns.

ULTRA supports

  • Building of customizable scenarios with different levels of difficulty
  • Analysis tools to evaluate traffic designs
  • Configurable train and test parameters
  • Baseline implementations of common RL algorithms
  • Benchmark results to train and test custom RL algorithms against

Task

For training, your goal is to design an agent that is capable of driving safely and efficiently across a variety of simulated intersection maps. Competitors will create (and optionally train) a driverless car from scratch or from one of the provided baselines, that is capable of traversing multi-lane intersections both safely and efficiently. Success will require the creation and/or training of intelligent driving behaviours which allow for precise manipulation of the car's controls, and account for the behaviour of nearby vehicles.

For evaluation, we are following a scoring metric that takes into account whether agent completed the left-turn, how fast the agent completed the left-turn, and also how safely the agent acted while completing the left-turn. We will evaluate your agent on hidden evaluation scenarios and determine a score for your agent submission. These hidden evaluation scenarios may range in the number of lanes, type of traffic density, seeding, and road speed. Though they differ in these aspects, they remain the same in the fact that all evaluation scenarios will be intersections that require your agent to undergo an unprotected left-turn. See the evaluation section for more information.

Procedure

Your challenge is to come up with an agent that can complete as many of the evaluation scenarios as quickly and as safely as it can. Your agent is allowed to use one of two observation types: either vector-based or image-based.

This competition provides two different tracks: Track 1 and Track 2. Evaluation scenarios will differ depending on which track you have submitted your agent to.

  • Track 1 will evaluate your agent on its ability to complete an unprotected left-turn with no traffic. That is, your agent must learn how to drive and complete a left-turn, but does not have to worry about collisions with other vehicles.
  • Track 2 is meant to push your agent to its limits. Like Track 1, Track 2 will evaluate your agent in unprotected left-turn scenarios, but this time with varying degrees of traffic in the intersection. That is, your agent must now not only be able to complete a left-turn, but it must also be mindful of neighboring vehicles and their movement.
